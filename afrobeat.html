Benga

Benga music, a popular genre of Kenyan music, originated in the late 1940s and evolved through the 1960s. It is a distinct style of guitar music that combines traditional Luo rhythms and instruments with other influences.

Today, top Benga singers include Key figures like: John Ogara: A pioneering guitarist who fused rural rhythms with urban music Daniel Owino Misiani: A standard bearer of benga from the 1970s until his death in 2006 Musa Juma: A well-known benga musician whose compositions have been the subject of scholarly works

Lingala

This style emerged in the Belgian Congo and French Middle Congo, fusing local and imported styles. The name comes from the musicians' love of Latin American styles, their fascination with rumba, and their practice of singing in Lingala.

Today, top Lingala singers include Early pioneers: Franco Luambo Makundu, Papa Wemba, Wendo Kolosoy, and Nico Kasanda established the genre's sound.
